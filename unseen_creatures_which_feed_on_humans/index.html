This spider is Myrmarachne plataleoides. It walks among the ferocious weaver ants and feeds upon them unchallenged. The ants never suspect. They are not equipped to recognize the danger. You might be thinking, "Humans are much smarter than ants. No creature could move among us unseen." Well of course germs and viruses do. But those are not the kind of creatures I am referring to. Still, in order to prepare you to perceive the creatures exposed in this article, it will help you to consider that only 150 years ago, most people didn't believe microorganisms existed.

In 1843, Dr. Oliver Wendell Holmes, Sr. (father of the Supreme Court judge) published The Contagiousness of Puerperal Fever, which argued, physicians and nurses frequently carried childbed fever, a deadly disease of women giving birth, from patient to patient.

A few years later (1847), Dr. Ignaz Semmelweiss, while employed at the maternity clinic of the Vienna General Hospital in Austria, introduced hand washing for his interns. This reduced the incidence of fatal childbed fever from 10 percent to about 1 percent. Semmelweiss' hypothesis was that pathogens too small to be seen remained on the interns' hands after conducting autopsies and that these pathogens were causing the deaths of his patients.

Holmes, seeing even more clearly than Semmelweiss proposed the existence of microorganisms and suggested that they were causing the disease. His famous essay anticipated Pasteur's discovery of Germ Theory later in the century.

The medical community was not only doubtful but also enraged at the idea that they themselves were introducing the pathogens that were killing their patients. Holmes and Semmelweiss were largely ignored, rejected and ridiculed. Semmelweiss was dismissed from the hospital, harassed by the medical community and was eventually forced to leave Vienna. He died a broken man in a mental institution only two years before Louis Pasteur did his famous experiments proving that microorganisms existed and that they in fact did cause disease.

With this understanding of the skepticism toward microorganisms only 150 years ago, you may now be willing to consider the existence of Macroorganisms. I am addressing one now as you read this. Not you - you are an organism. You are made up of various cells all descended from the same fertilized egg. All of your cells work together sharing materials and information while each one is specialized for a specific task. A macroorganism is made up of various organisms which work together in much the same way your cells do. As I publish this document to the Internet and optimize it's rating on Google, I am also addressing the public in its entirety as a single intelligent living organism of which you and I are a part. It has a lot of names. Invisible Hand, Complex Adaptive System, Distributed Consciousness, Swarm Intelligence, Superorganism, Hyperbeing, Hive Mind, World Market, Collective Consciousness, and Macroorganism are a few of those names. Let's call it Mac for short.

To understand who Mac is and what I am trying to do, just imagine me as a single human brain cell trying to communicate with the human mind which emerges as the side effect of me and all my brother and sister brain cells conducting our personal business. This business consisting mostly of trading chemicals and chattering with the brain cells we happen to be directly connected to. The rich complexity of this human mind results from the fact that no two brain cells are connected to their neighbors in exactly the same way and yet every cell is able to communicate with every other by passing messages through intermediary cells. This explains our complexity, but what about consciousness? There are lots of complex systems that aren't conscious. We are conscious because our brain cells are connected in feedback loops. That means that the things Herbie the Brain Cell says to its neighbors affect the things the neighbors say back to Herbie which in turn affects the things that Herbie says to his neighbors. And so it is with people who are connected to other people in feedback loops but none connected in exactly the same way and yet somehow all able to communicate with each other through intermediary people and through media like books, television and now the Internet. Mac emerges from all this connected chatter.

If you don't believe that Mac exists that's quite understandable. After all, where is he? Well where exactly is your consciousness? That question has no meaning because if consciousness isn't moving it ceases to exist. Consciousness cannot be found in any of your brain cells. It's the information (pulses of electricity and electrochemical potentials) that bounce between the cells. Give someone anesthesia (that is to say block the exchange of electrochemical energy between brain cells) and we have unconsciousness. Consciousness is energy moving in complex interconnected feedback loops. Let's call it a "feedback web" so we don't have to keep saying "complex" and "interconnected". It's energy so it's hard to see, its movements are too complex to track, and it ceases to exist when it stops moving. Yes, consciousness is hard to comprehend, but to imagine that the brain is the only place where feedback webs occur is not rational, helpful, or as we shall see, safe.

I'll bet you're wondering; "If Mac is conscious and alive, how come he never talks to us"? Well you don't you talk to your brain cells do you? Of course not; instead you might have a glass of wine so that they will stop talking to you. There are three main reasons we don't try to communicate with our brain cells. The first is that brain cells are very simple compared to us and so they think about very simple things like "How much neurotransmitter am I receiving" and "Should I release my store of neurotransmitters to other brain cells?" So we don't have much to discuss with our brain cells. Secondly, we are interested in very different things. We normally trade in money, food, or sex not neurotransmitters. The third reason we don't communicate with our brain cells is that we think at different speeds. It only takes a couple of milliseconds for a brain cell to decide whether or not to release its store of neurotransmitters but it may take us 5 minutes or more to figure out how much money to tip the waiter. In any case, it seems likely that these constraints on conversation also apply to our relationship with Mac where humans think at a faster rate than Mac but with a much smaller range of ideas and seemingly no common interests. Well, actually we do have common a interest. Mac's well-being as an individual is completely dependent on our well-being as a group. And our well-being as a group is dependent on Macs well-being as an individual. Furthermore, we share a common enemy as well. That is the subject of this article, but we're not prepared to discuss that just yet.

Miracles are happening all around us like a fireworks display at every moment in response to our needs, our questions, the things we ask for, and the things we want. There is never a moment when God is not focused on each and every one of us, but few people even notice. That's because we are not paying attention to him. We must be paying attention to God in order to more fully receive the blessings he showers upon us. This is what we are really praying for when asking for God's greatest gift (The Holy Spirit) - that we are fully present and paying attention to him as he speaks his blessings upon us. For most of us, our thinking about the Holy Spirit is incomplete and so dysfunctional. It's a two way connection which also requires our attention in order to work. It's not only something he gives to us but also something which we must deliberately allow in order fully receive his gifts. Talking with God, reading scripture, sitting quietly, remembering our blessings, writing down our thoughts, feeling thankful, and pleasantly imagining the things we want, all help us to pay attention as God speaks miracles to us.

What is the Soul?

We can know exactly what the soul is by considering context from God's One Great Commandment.
Jesus tells us:
Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind.
We know "with all thy heart" means that we are to choose to feel, from our range of possible emotions, those which are loving towards God.
We know "with all thy mind" means that we are to choose to think, from our range of possible thoughts, those which are loving towards God.
The above two are internal, they happen inside us.
There is only one thing left, and this one is external. It's action.
From context we know that "with all thy soul" means that we are to choose to do, from our range of possible actions, those which are loving towards God.
So the soul is the set of all actions that we can choose to take in any given moment.
It's our state of mind.
And isn't that what everybody is worried about at the moment of death?
"God save my soul" we cry.
We are asking God to save our current state of mind, or to say it another way, save our current range of choices.
The size and shape of your soul (your range of choices) is always changing over time.
The choices we make now exert enormous control over the range of choices we have in the future.
And our current thoughts and emotions exert enormous control over the range of choices we have in the present.
So our thoughts and emotions define our range of possible actions, which is to say that our thoughts and emotions define our state of mind, or to say it again, our thoughts and emotions define our souls.

It's easy to see now that loving thoughts and emotions tend towards life, where choices grow, and by definition also grows the soul.
It's also plain to see that fearful, angry, lustful thoughts and emotions tend towards death, where choices diminish and so withers the soul.
Addiction is slavery by definition. It's a condition where there is little choice and so again withers the soul.
The key point is:
A fearful, angry, lustful, addicted person, has a completely different range of choices than a loving person has even if you put them in exactly the same situation.
That's because the state of mind (the soul) is different.
So our soul requires that we entertain loving thoughts and emotions while rejecting fear, anger, lust, and addiction.
When this is achieved we call the soul pure.
We are typically born with pure souls which quickly become contaminated with fear, anger, lust, and addiction.
This is due to contamination in every environment where there is always some abuse, poor teaching, and lack of care.
Purifying the soul again and then keeping it pure is extremely difficult, but living with a contaminated soul is many times more difficult due to the pain it causes.
So after enough pain, everybody will seek purification.
Talking with God, reading scripture, sitting quietly, writing down our thoughts, caring for ourselves and caring for others, help us to purify the soul and to keep it that way.

The soul is immortal:
The current state of any computer can be saved and then accessed at a later time, on the same machine, or on a different one.
God does this with us. We know because we save the states of the people in our own dreams, and then allow them to continue in later dreams.

What Is Love

The word love as used in God's One Great Commandment found in Matthew 22:35-40: is respect for the wellbeing and autonomy of the self or another combined with the willingness to act. Loving people have two of the three elements required to commit an act of love. The final requirement is any visible need as this brings form, purpose and release to a loving persons respectful ideas and willingness to act.

The smallest unit of love is the gift of a binary choice (this or that) given to the self or to another for one to decide.
It's the gift of a new possibility space or another dimension of autonomy.

Praying For Good Things

Be extremely careful when asking God for things.
God answers all our prayers but it is very rare that people ask for good things.
Better to pray as Jesus teaches in Luke Chapter 11:
First Jesus tells us our relationship with God and where he is (Our Father which art in Heaven). More on that here
Then Jesus tells us to ask for the following:
That we all come to know and love God (Hallowed be thy name). This is for our benefit not his - think about it.
That we bring Heaven to Earth by doing God's will (Thy kingdom come thy will be done). What specifically God wants us to do is found here
That our faith in God displaces all fear and leaves our minds free to focus on the requirements and the joys that this moment brings (Give us day by day our daily bread).
That we can forgive (let go), so that our hands are open and ready to receive God's constant blessings (And forgive us our sins; for we also forgive every one that is indebted to us).
That we be blessed with the good sense to stay away from addictive and evil things (And lead us not into temptation; but deliver us from evil).
And that He continues to bless us with the Holy Spirit - His love and attention on us and ours looking back at Him (If ye then, being evil, know how to give good gifts unto your children: how much more shall your heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to them that ask him?).
Thank you Jesus for showing us our inheritance and for teaching us what to ask for.

Typically we speak to God in words and he answers back in miracles. But words are not even necessary for prayer and often cut us off from the Holy Spirit making it difficult to receive the gifts that God showers upon us. It is much better to quietly meditate in a state of appreciation for the things we have and to pleasantly imagine the things we want. All thought is prayer and eventually we get what we pray for. Because all thought is prayer, meditating in a state of regret for the things that are missing in our lives or asking for avoidance of the things we don't want will only bring more emptiness and unwanted things. Eventually, we get what we think about so think deliberately about the things which bring joy, or even better, don't think much at all but rather appreciate the joy that God brings.

If thought is prayer then you may wonder, How can we know what is good to think about?
You can tell by how your thoughts make you feel. Thoughts which bring joy with a sense of calm or perhaps anticipation will bring you the things that you want. Thoughts that make you feel anxiety, sadness, anger, fear, or a sense of meanness will bring you destruction. So pay attention to how you feel while you are thinking and think deliberately.

The very best prayer is a combination of meditation and thankfulness, while sometimes pleasantly imagining the things we want.

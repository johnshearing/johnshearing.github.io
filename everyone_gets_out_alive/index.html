Since he would be flying without an instructor on this day for the first time he would need to be 100% focused. This was not a day to have distractions. Lisa expected that I would be at the airport with Mac on that day so she sent sheriff’s officers to serve me with court papers demanding child support and alimony payments or face jail time. I was not at the airport but rather outside of the airport positioned for taking photographs of the occasion. All pilots are required to make a self-evaluation before each flight to consider if they are focused enough to make the flight safely. Even with the distractions and stress of having sheriff’s officers show up looking for his father, Mac felt that he could safely make the flight. I didn't know that this was going on at the time. I learned of it after. From where I was standing I could see that Mac made a perfect take off and a perfect landing. I was so proud of him on that day and even more proud when I learned that he was able to keep his focus despite the arrival of the sheriff’s officers sent by Lisa. That was pretty much the last straw for Mac. Lisa had selfishly increased the risk of an accident on a day that he really needed to focus. To make things worse, Lisa had a new boyfriend who had recently threatened to knock out Mac's teeth. Mac was not afraid of the new boyfriend but he didn't need the drama. He was now 16 years old. That was old enough to fly alone, old enough to be heard in the courts, and old enough to decide for himself where he wanted to live.

Right after Mac finished his first solo flight I went back to work driving my taxi. The sheriff's officers caught up with me there and delivered the summons for court. I went to court at the appointed time and was told by the judge that I would be making payments to the probation department for child support or I would be going to jail. Stupidly and selfishly I told the judge that I would not live as a slave to anybody and that I preferred jail. This was the worst thing I could have said. I was so close to winning freedom and a stable home for Mac and I was about to throw it all away. Fortunately, this judge was not quick to anger. The judge told me that she would give me a week to think about it. In the meantime I was given instructions on how to make payments. It didn't take me long to realize that I had better stop playing from the old play book and come up with a new one. I asked my brother Geoff for a loan to pay the probation department about $100 per week while I worked on a new plan. He was happy to help.

About two weeks later Lisa and a new lawyer filed a motion asking the courts to put me in jail, to confiscate my driver's license, and to attach Angela's wages. They didn't know that Angela was providing childcare services on a volunteer basis. She wasn't being paid. Today she still freely and lovingly cares for the same young man as if he were her own son. Anyway it looked like this time there would be a good chance that I would be jailed before I had a chance to break Mac free. It looked like the beast would devour us both after all. I wrote a brief explaining everything to the judge about what I was doing for my son and why I should not be jailed but honestly, I didn't expect to be heard.

A couple of days before the hearing I was still seeing my son for dinner as I had every day for the past 8 or so years. He mentioned to me that Lisa's entire family was in town. I figured they came to see me humiliated in court and finally jailed after all these years. That night a generous gay black couple got into my taxi. These were two large powerful men who earned their living as humble washroom attendants in an upscale New York club. It seemed like a contradiction. As in my situation, most of their earnings came from the generosity of others, so they understood. They only lived a couple of blocks away from the train station but they always gave the drivers a $20 tip for helping them with their bags. You can't possibly know how much that helps unless you are trying to support a family on an average wage of $5 per hour. These two men always carry the Holy Spirit but on this night especially they were angels carrying a message from God himself. We often talked because these two men truly cared about other people. They asked me how I was feeling and how my son was doing. I told them that I thought I would be going to jail in a few days for non-payment of child support. They told me that they were sorry and they gave me $20 like always but this time they gave me a book too. The book was called the Power Of Now by Eckhart Tolle. Linked here is a very quick YouTube summary which conveys the key ideas. If you play the video at double speed you can get through it in about 8 minutes and still understand it all. Anyway, I devoured the book and felt a peace come over me as I was reading it. I knew then what I had allowed myself to forget after all these years - That God was here. That was the message they were carrying.

There was a carnival atmosphere when I arrived at court. I was alone and representing myself. Lisa had a fresh lawyer and was surrounded by her entire family. They looked at me with excitement, like they were at a banquet and I was the animal to be slaughtered and roasted for their pleasure. I was at such peace. God was in this room with us and nobody else could feel it. Then Judge Daniel R. Lindemann walked into the courtroom. He looked like a man always aware of God's presence. He seemed revolted by what he saw in his courtroom and immediately admonished Lisa and her family for turning this child welfare matter into a circus. After order was established we all had our turn to speak. Lisa demanded that I continue making payments to the probation department and that if I missed just two more payments that I be jailed, my driver's license be confiscated and that my wife's wages be garnished (again, my wife was a volunteer). When it was my turn to speak I told the judge that my son needed my support and I asked the judge to allow me to raise my son unmolested by Lisa and her lawyers until my son was out of flight school and out of college. Now it was the judges turn to speak. He then told me that he could see I loved my son and acknowledged that I was supporting him. Then he explained to me that the law required that the judgements for child support to Lisa must be satisfied. He said he understood from my brief that I was asking for the opportunity to care for my son but I didn't make the request in a way that the law could accommodate. He did make it clear, however, that the law works, and that it is here to solve family problems. He then held up a book of law and advised me to read it. He said pay attention to the laws regarding child support and to come back asking for help in a way that the law can respond to. Then he scheduled another hearing in two weeks and dismissed the court.

Thank you God for sending us a judge that could hear you. Judge Lidenemann indicated that there was a way to stay in Mac's life. "The law works", he promised. The answer was in the book he held up. The text of the book was available online so I didn't have to order it and then wait for it to arrive - I could get started right away. I looked under the section about child support and there was the answer. Under N.J.S.A. 2A:34-23 it stated that Lisa was on the hook for one half of Mac's flight training and for one half of his college. Just half of the flight training alone was much more than I owed to her in back child support. It turned out that she owed me money - a lot of money. This was money I had no intention of collecting but I now had a lever I could use to break Mac free. So I wrote a motion to the court asking for help in a way that the court could respond to. I asked the court to grant Mac's request to come live with me and to require Lisa to pay for half of Mac's flight training and for half of his college. Finally, I asked that since Lisa is now the one that owes child support, the probation department should no longer be involved. Lisa understood that she was now on the hook for several hundred thousand dollars. In order to get out from under the debt she filed a cross motion of her own agreeing to call it even on the child support and to allow Mac to live with Me and Angela.

We went back to court and stood before Judge Lindemann to formalize everything. As I was leaving the court Judge Lindemann called to me in a commanding voice, "Mr. Shearing". "Yes Your Honor", I responed. "Mr. Shearing, love your son". "Yes Your Honor, I will", I said back. That was the first time I ever noticed God speaking in a human voice.
